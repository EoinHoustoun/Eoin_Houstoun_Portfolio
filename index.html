About

Professional Summary

I'm an Irish Data Scientist and AI Engineer with an MSc (Distinction) in AI & Data Science from Goldsmiths, University of London, and a First Class BSc in Data Science & Analytics from University College Cork. My research on Alzheimer's disease classification was accepted and published at the AIAI 2025 International Conference, featured in Springer's Artificial Intelligence Applications and Innovations - a peer-reviewed venue at the forefront of applied AI research.

I'm currently an AI Engineer at TurinTech, working on Artemis — a code optimisation product that makes LLM inference faster and cheaper for engineering teams — building auto-discovery tooling, multi-agent systems with the Claude API, and LLM benchmarking frameworks. I specialise in end-to-end ML systems: from feature engineering and rigorous validation to SHAP-based interpretability and deployment.

Research

Published Work

Peer Reviewed Springer AIAI 2025

Classifying Cognitive States of Alzheimer's Disease with Machine Learning Using Digital Biomarkers from the Bio-Hermes Study Cohort

Eoin Houstoun et al.
Artificial Intelligence Applications and Innovations - AIAI 2025
IFIP Advances in Information and Communication Technology · Springer · 2025

This paper presents a machine learning approach to classifying individuals into cognitive states of Alzheimer's disease using the Bio-Hermes dataset. Employing XGBoost, Random Forest, and Monte Carlo cross-validation with rigorous feature selection, the work demonstrates high-accuracy classification from digital biomarker data, advancing clinical AI decision-support for neurodegenerative conditions.
